This folder contains the engineering projects portfolio of Dr. Eng. Lamice Denguir organized as follows:

01-ENIM 2010: This folder contains full reports and presentations of mini-projects done as team work 
between 2010 and 2012 when Lamice Denguir was a graduate student at the National School of Engineering of Monastir. 
All documentation is written in french and is object to ISO and NF standards. It contains 4 projects: 

  *Project 2010 Gearbox: This mini-project presents the detailed design methodology for a mechanical system. 
  The objective of this study is to develop the complete technical design documentation for a speed reducer that 
  meets a given set of design specifications. To achieve this objective, we applied our knowledge of mechanics 
  of materials, technical drawing and engineering communication, and computer-aided design (CAD). 
  We also relied on scientific references for stress and strength calculations, manufacturers' catalogs for 
  the selection of standardized and commercially available components, as well as the indispensable *Mechanical 
  Drafting Guide* to ensure compliance with engineering drawing standards.

  *Project 2011 Nacelle: This project presents the complete mechanical design and control system development 
  of a hydraulic scissor lift platform, covering requirements analysis, engineering calculations, CAD modeling, 
  structural analysis, and control system design. The selected case study was the design of a scissor lift 
  platform for several reasons.
  First, although the operating principle of the mechanism appears relatively simple, its design requires the 
  integration of knowledge from numerous engineering disciplines, including design methodology, mechanics of 
  materials, finite element analysis (FEA), tribology, structural analysis, machine design, materials 
  engineering,  hydraulics, computer-aided design (CAD), automation, and industrial computing. Second, 
  the project provided an opportunity to gain practical experience with professional engineering software for
  modeling, simulation, and programming, including RdM6, ABAQUS/CAE, CATIA V5, ISIS Proteus, and the PICC
  Compiler. These software tools significantly accelerated the design process, allowing the entire project to 
  be completed within a two-month period.
  
  *Project 2011 Powder: This literature review project was organized into the following chapters:
  – Natural Resources Related to Powder and Bulk Solids Processing in Tunisia:** This chapter presents the geographical distribution of Tunisia's natural resources used in powder and bulk solids processing. It also provides an overview of the country's powder processing industries, including the cement, gypsum, calcium carbonate, and chemical industries, as well as manufacturing activities that rely on imported raw materials.
  – Equipment for the Storage and Handling of Powders and Bulk Solids:** This chapter reviews the industrial equipment used for the storage of powdered and granular materials according to particle size classification. It also describes the material handling systems employed in industry, including conveyors and discharge equipment used for the transport and extraction of bulk solids.
  – Dust Collection Systems in Industrial Production Facilities:** This chapter explains the importance of dust collection in industrial plants, the design principles of industrial dust collectors, and their operating mechanisms. Particular attention is given to their role in maintaining process efficiency, ensuring workplace safety, and meeting environmental regulations.

   *Project 2012 Devidoir: This project presents the design of the film unwinding unit of a food packaging machine. The complete packaging system consists of three main modules: the film winding and printing unit, the film unwinding unit, and the product filling system. In this project, the unwinding unit is treated as an independent mechanical subsystem. The design process began with a literature review and the development of the functional design specifications, followed by the evaluation of alternative design concepts and detailed engineering calculations. The project culminated in the production of the complete technical design documentation for the subsystem. We delivered a Detailed Mechanical Design (Presents the engineering calculations used to size the mechanical components and justifies the selection of appropriate materials) and Technical Design Documentation (Presents the final design deliverables, including the 3D CAD model, assembly drawings, and detailed manufacturing drawings of the designed components).

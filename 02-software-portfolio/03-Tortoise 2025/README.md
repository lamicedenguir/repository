# 🐢 Tortoise

> **Read. Share. Grow.**

"This project demonstrates my ability to use AI-assisted development to rapidly prototype, customize, debug, and deploy a production-ready web application while ensuring security, legal compliance, and user experience." Dr. Eng. Lamice Denguir.

Tortoise is a free educational web application that encourages children to develop a love for reading by keeping track of the books they read, sharing reviews with friends, and participating in reading challenges in a safe online environment.

🌐 **Live Application:** https://tortoise.lovable.app/

---

## About the Project

Tortoise was inspired by my daughter, **Mariam Inès Denguir do Outeiro**, who wanted a fun way to share her passion for books with her classmates, friends, and cousins living overseas.

I transformed her idea into a fully functional web application designed specifically for young readers.

The platform promotes reading while maintaining a child-friendly experience based on three simple principles:

- 📚 Reading comes first.
- 🛡️ Safety and privacy are essential.
- 🚫 No advertising, no tracking, no commercialization.

---

## Features

### 📖 Reading Journal

- Log every book you read
- Record reading time
- Rate books
- Write personal reviews

### 👥 Friends

- Connect with friends
- Share reading activity
- Comment respectfully on books

### 🏆 Reading Challenges

- Create reading challenges
- Participate with friends
- Stay motivated through shared goals

### 🌍 Child-Friendly Community

- Designed for children between **5 and 17 years old**
- Parent or guardian permission required
- Respectful and moderated environment

---

## Privacy First

Unlike many social platforms aimed at young audiences, Tortoise was designed with privacy as a priority.

The application:

- ✅ contains **no advertisements**
- ✅ does **not sell user data**
- ✅ uses **no tracking or analytics cookies**
- ✅ only uses essential authentication cookies
- ✅ complies with COPPA and GDPR principles

## Design Philosophy

The interface was intentionally designed to be:

- simple enough for children
- colorful and welcoming
- responsive on desktop and mobile
- easy to navigate without distractions

The goal is to keep the focus on books rather than screens.
